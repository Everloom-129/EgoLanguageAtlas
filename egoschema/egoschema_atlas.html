EgoSchema, Mangalam et al., NeurIPS 2023 (built on Ego4D)

EgoSchema is a diagnostic long-form video question-answering benchmark of 5,031 five-way multiple-choice questions over about 3-minute Ego4D clips, more than 250 hours in total. Each question was first generated by large language models from dense Ego4D narrations, producing a question, a correct answer, and hard distractors, then human-curated so that answering demands a long temporal certificate (the minimum span a human must watch). The questions and five options are released openly, but the correct-answer key is withheld and models are scored only through a public leaderboard. Because the data is built on Ego4D and released under the Ego4D License, the strings are linked rather than redistributed here.

04Why this is a descriptive entry

Verified via arXiv 2308.09126, egoschema.github.io, and github.com/egoschema/EgoSchema. Built on Ego4D and released under the Ego4D License.

EgoSchema releases its question and option strings openly, but it is a benchmark built on Ego4D and inherits the Ego4D License, the correct-answer key is withheld, and scoring runs through a public leaderboard. As an Ego4D-derived benchmark whose text carries the gated license, it is documented descriptively with links to the source rather than embedded as a redistributed corpus.
