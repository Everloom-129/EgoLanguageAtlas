DeMiAn (How to Instruct Your Robot), Kim et al., arXiv:2605.17077, 2026

DeMiAn (Dense Multi-Aspect Annotation) treats language density as a lever to extract more signal from fixed corpora without collecting new data. A vision-language model (Qwen3-VL) re-labels existing demonstration segments along four aspects, physical motion, scene composition, arm pose, and reasoning, over more than one million robot clips and about 50,000 EgoVerse human egocentric videos. A small learned instructor then selects which annotation aspect to inject at deployment, run asynchronously so generation latency hides behind the rollout. The richer language improves both a VLA policy and a video-based world-action model, but the dense annotations themselves are not released.

04Why this is a descriptive entry

Verified via arXiv 2605.17077 (paper CC BY 4.0). No code, project page, or dense annotations released as of the access date; source data is >1M robot clips plus ~50K EgoVerse human videos.

DeMiAn is the clearest dense-multi-aspect annotation exemplar, but it is a method, not a released corpus. The paper is CC BY 4.0 and no code, data, or dense annotations have been published, and the work re-labels other datasets (robot clips and EgoVerse) rather than originating a standalone corpus. It is therefore documented descriptively until any annotations are released.
