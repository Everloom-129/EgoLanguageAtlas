Phantom, Lepert et al., CoRL 2025

Phantom records human demonstrations with a single fixed RGBD camera placed at the robot's future viewpoint, then estimates the demonstrator's hand pose to derive gripper actions. It edits every frame by segmenting and inpainting out the human arm and overlaying a rendered robot arm, closing the visual embodiment gap so human video looks like robot video. The resulting imitation policy deploys zero-shot on real hardware. Phantom releases code and trained policies, not a language-annotation corpus.

04Why this is a descriptive entry

Verified via arXiv 2503.00779, github.com/MarionLepert/phantom (MIT), and phantom-human-videos.github.io. The camera is fixed at the robot viewpoint, not head-mounted.

Phantom is a human-to-robot imitation method, not an annotation source. Its supervision comes from extracted hand poses and edited frames rather than text, and it releases code (MIT) and policies, not a new language corpus. This atlas documents Phantom descriptively rather than clustering downloaded strings.
