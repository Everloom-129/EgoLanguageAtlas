01Data-usage path

Example egocentric media
Ego4D is a worldwide first-person corpus; the project page shows a teaser video and a collage of egocentric frames.
example frame · example video · project page
Ego4D, Grauman et al., CVPR 2022

Ego4D collects 3,670 hours of daily-life first-person video from more than 900 camera wearers across 74 locations in 9 countries. Annotators write dense, timestamped free-form narrations (about 3.85 million sentences) describing each atomic action, using a tag convention where #C marks the camera wearer and #O, #X, #Y mark other people in the scene. These narrations seed task-specific labels for a multi-task benchmark covering episodic memory, hands and objects, audio-visual diarization, social interaction, and forecasting. The video and narrations sit behind a signed license and are fetched with the ego4d CLI, so the raw narration text cannot be redistributed in this atlas.

04Why this is a descriptive entry

Verified via arXiv 2110.07058, ego4d-data.org, and github.com/facebookresearch/Ego4d. Dataset gated under the Ego4D License (code MIT).

Ego4D is the canonical download+cluster target, but its language sits behind a signed dataset license that restricts redistribution. The narrations can be analyzed locally after approval, yet the verbatim strings cannot be republished here, so Ego4D is documented descriptively with links to the gated source rather than an embedded clusterable corpus.
